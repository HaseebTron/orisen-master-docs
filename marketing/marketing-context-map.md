# Marketing Context Map

## Purpose of this file

This file is the routing map for the `marketing/` folder.

Use it to decide which marketing docs to read for messaging, positioning, content, channel strategy, GTM, customer language, launch, waitlist growth, and founder-led marketing questions.

This file does not replace marketing source-of-truth docs. It points readers and ChatGPT projects to the right marketing docs.

## Always read first

For important marketing work, start with:

- `ai-context/current-state.md`
- `ai-context/source-of-truth-rules.md`
- `product/product-overview.md`
- `product/claims-and-evidence.md`
- `product/target-customer.md`
- `marketing/positioning-and-messaging.md`

Marketing must stay downstream from current company truth, product direction, target customer assumptions, and claims/evidence boundaries.

## Marketing folder role

The `marketing/` folder defines:

- Marketing strategy
- Messaging and positioning for external audiences
- Customer language and pain points
- Founder-led content strategy
- Channel-specific playbooks
- GTM strategy
- Waitlist growth and conversion strategy
- Launch and prelaunch strategy
- Research from Reddit, TikTok, X, LinkedIn, YouTube, reviews, and customer conversations

Marketing docs should translate product truth into compelling external messaging without inventing unsupported capabilities.

## Marketing interpretation rules

- Marketing is downstream from product truth and claims evidence.
- Do not position Orisen as generic sleep wellness.
- Do not reduce Orisen to just a smart alarm clock.
- Do not overclaim sleep-stage accuracy, grogginess elimination, or clinical sleep improvement.
- The strongest validated wedge is presence-based wake completion.
- Grogginess reduction and sleep-phase-aware intervention are strategically important, but claims must remain careful until validated.
- Messaging should focus on painful mornings, chronic oversleeping, sleeping through alarms, getting out of bed, and waking less brutally.
- Customer language should influence marketing more than abstract wellness language.

## Default marketing framing

Unless a newer source-of-truth file says otherwise, Orisen marketing should frame the product as:

A non-contact sleep intervention device for people whose mornings are broken: it helps them actually get out of bed and is being built toward less groggy, less brutal wake-ups through gradual, sensor-informed intervention.

The first marketing wedge should be specific and painful, not generic:

- Oversleeping
- Sleeping through alarms
- Turning alarms off and going back to bed
- Waking up brutally groggy
- Feeling like normal alarms and willpower do not work

The product should be marketed as a system for fixing painful mornings, not as another sleep tracker.
