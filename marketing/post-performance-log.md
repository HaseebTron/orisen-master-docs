# Post Performance Log

## Purpose

This file tracks founder-led marketing posts, launch posts, content experiments, and campaign performance.

Use it to preserve:

- exact post copy
- creative details
- distribution actions
- performance metrics
- derived metrics
- marketing lessons
- what likely worked
- what was weak or uncertain
- follow-up content experiments

This file is a marketing learning log. It is not the same as `validation/evidence-log.md`.

## Relationship to evidence docs

Use this file for detailed marketing analysis and execution learning.

Use `validation/evidence-log.md` for conservative evidence classification and claim boundaries.

A post can show message resonance, audience interest, or content-channel signal without proving willingness to pay, product-market fit, retention, clinical efficacy, or technical efficacy.

Do not strengthen product, fundraising, or public claims from this file alone. Check:

- `validation/evidence-log.md`
- `validation/evidence-standard.md`
- `product/claims-and-evidence.md`

## 2026-02-04 — Founder LinkedIn waitlist launch post

### Context

- Platform: LinkedIn.
- Account: Founder personal LinkedIn account.
- Post type: Founder launch / waitlist announcement / collaborator call.
- Posted: 2026-02-04 at about 2:15 PM.
- Goal:
  - Drive early awareness.
  - Drive waitlist signups.
  - Test whether the painful wake-up problem and Orisen concept resonate.
  - Invite potential cofounders or collaborators to reach out.
- Hypothesis:
  - A pain-first founder post about waking up on time, snoozing alarms, and brutal mornings would resonate enough to generate organic attention and waitlist signups.
- Audience:
  - Founder network and extended LinkedIn audience.
  - Likely mixed audience, not purely cold target customers.

### Exact post copy

```text
Imagine there was a foolproof way to get you to wake up on time. 
Imagine getting out of bed in the morning didn’t feel like a mission.

You snoozing through your 8 alarms and it feels like a mission to pull yourself out of bed every morning.
You want to fix your sleep schedule but nothing works.
And this happens 100’s of times a year.

I couldn’t find a solution to all this, so I built one.

Orisen is a sleep intervention device that fixes your sleep for you. It use sensors, a smart feedback loop, audio/light probing to influence your sleep and fix waking up.

Check out the website here: orisen.ai
Join the 𝐰𝐚𝐢𝐭𝐥𝐢𝐬𝐭 for early access and discounted prices.

I’m also looking for co-founders and people to collaborate with, shoot me a DM.
```

### What likely worked

- The post led with a concrete pain rather than a generic product announcement.
- The hook focused on waking up on time and getting out of bed not feeling like a mission.
- The copy referenced a familiar failure mode: snoozing through many alarms.
- The post framed the problem as repeated and painful: happening hundreds of times per year.
- The founder-build framing made the post feel personal and credible.
- The waitlist CTA was direct.
- Early friend likes likely helped initial distribution.
- Fast replies to comments likely increased discussion and kept the post active.
- The collaborator/cofounder line may have widened the audience beyond only customers.

### What was weak or uncertain

- The line "fixes your sleep for you" is too strong for current evidence and should not become approved source-of-truth wording.
- The line "audio/light probing to influence your sleep" may be too technical or unusual for normal customers.
- The phrase "foolproof way" could be interpreted as an overclaim if reused in external marketing.
- Friend likes and founder-network distribution mean the result is not purely cold-market validation.
- Waitlist signups do not prove willingness to pay.
- The post does not reveal which exact customer segment is most willing to pay.
- The post does not validate grogginess reduction, sleep-stage-aware intervention, radar-based sensing, or product efficacy.
- Because the post also asked for collaborators/cofounders, some engagement may reflect startup curiosity rather than customer demand.

### Claim boundary

Supports:
- Early organic interest in Orisen from founder-led LinkedIn distribution.
- Message resonance around painful wake-ups, oversleeping, and getting out of bed.
- A promising early landing-page/waitlist conversion signal under current tracking assumptions.
- Founder-led LinkedIn may be a useful early distribution and discovery channel.

Does not support:
- Willingness to pay.
- Product-market fit.
- Broad-market demand.
- Retention.
- Clinical efficacy.
- Reduced grogginess.
- Reduced sleep inertia.
- Sleep-stage-aware intervention.
- Artificial sleep phase transitioning.
- Radar-based sleep-stage estimation.
- That all signups came from LinkedIn alone.

### Lessons

- Repeat the pain-first framing around waking up on time, snoozing alarms, and getting out of bed.
- Keep the simplest customer-facing promise close to: wake up reliably and make mornings less brutal.
- Preserve founder-led storytelling, but tighten claims discipline.
- Avoid implying that Orisen already fixes all sleep or controls sleep stages.
- Separate customer-facing language from technical explanation. Normal users may not need phrases like "audio/light probing."
- Track post timing, early engagement actions, replies, link clicks, site visits, and signups for every future post.

### Follow-up experiments

- Test a post focused only on the pain of snoozing many alarms and going back to bed.
- Test a post focused only on "getting out of bed" as the core behavioral problem.
- Test a post focused on waking up feeling brutally groggy, while keeping claims careful.
- Test a build-in-public post with prototype visuals and a narrower CTA.
- Test a customer-discovery CTA instead of a waitlist CTA.
- Test a post that asks people with severe wake-up problems to comment or DM their morning routine failure mode.
- Test a clearer product explanation using language like "built to make waking up reliable and less brutal" instead of "fixes your sleep for you."
