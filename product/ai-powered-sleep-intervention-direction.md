# AI-Powered Sleep Intervention Direction

## Purpose

This file captures the product/future-direction update that emerged from the Founders Inc positioning work.

It is not a marketing-copy file. It defines the product truth that downstream marketing, fundraising, and roadmap language should stay consistent with.

## Current status

This direction is a long-term product architecture direction.

It does not mean Orisen currently has:

- validated AI sleep agents
- validated autonomous sleep intervention
- validated vital-sign sensing
- validated sleep-stage sensing
- validated conversational sleep coaching
- clinically validated sleep outcomes

Wake completion remains the first wedge and the strongest current product evidence.

## Product direction summary

Orisen's long-term direction is a non-wearable bedroom hardware layer for AI-powered sleep intervention.

The device should combine non-contact sensing, local compute, audio, light, voice interaction, connectivity, and local control so software-defined sleep interventions can sense context and act in the bedroom.

Wake completion remains the first software-defined intervention. Future layers may include sensor-informed wake intervention, voice-guided sleep/wake support, sleep onset support, personalized routines, optional wearable/health integrations, and broader AI-assisted sleep interventions.

## Hardware layer model

Orisen should be understood as a reusable hardware foundation for multiple sleep interventions over time.

The hardware layer may provide:

- non-contact sensing
- presence and movement context
- future vital-sign-related signals where technically feasible
- future sleep-stage-related signals where technically feasible and validated
- local compute
- local alarm execution
- local storage
- audio output
- light output
- voice interaction capability
- connectivity
- app/cloud support where useful
- backup power for alarm-critical windows

The hardware layer is not the whole promise by itself. The value comes from the combination of hardware, software, sensing, intervention logic, reliability, user trust, and validated outcomes.

## Software-defined intervention model

Orisen should be built as one hardware foundation with multiple software-defined intervention layers.

The first layer is wake completion:

- alarm execution
- presence-based bed-exit detection
- confirmation that the user got out of bed
- re-trigger behavior if the user returns to bed
- local reliability during the alarm-critical moment

Future software-defined intervention layers may include:

- gradual audio/light wake support
- sensor-informed wake timing
- sleep-phase-aware wake behavior if validated
- bedtime wind-down routines
- sleep onset support
- white noise and sleep-supportive audio
- sleep-supportive lighting
- schedule correction
- personalized routines
- intelligent voice interaction
- AI-assisted or autonomous sleep interventions

Each future intervention layer must be validated separately before strong claims are allowed.

## Voice interaction as an intervention channel

Voice should be treated as a possible intervention/output channel, not as the whole product identity.

Voice may eventually support:

- wake-up dialogue
- bedtime guidance
- contextual prompts
- explanation of what the device is doing
- deliberate user confirmation or override flows
- adaptive coaching around repeated wake failures or bedtime inconsistency

Voice should be:

- useful
- contextual
- optional where appropriate
- privacy-sensitive
- tested for user tolerance
- subordinate to reliable wake completion

Voice should not make Orisen sound like a generic smart speaker or chatbot in the bedroom.

## AI sleep-agent direction

An AI sleep agent should be understood as software that can use context, make decisions, and take actions through Orisen's hardware layer.

In future versions, an AI sleep agent might:

- understand the user's wake goal
- use schedule, history, presence, movement, and sleep-context signals
- decide which intervention to run
- adjust audio, light, voice, timing, and wake-completion behavior
- observe whether the intervention worked
- adapt the next routine or intervention

Orisen's role is to give sleep software and future AI agents a physical presence in the bedroom: sensing inputs and intervention outputs.

This is a future-facing platform direction, not a current validated capability.

## Non-wearable advanced sensing pillar

A major long-term product pillar is bringing advanced sleep sensing out of wearables and into the bedroom.

This may include:

- presence detection
- movement sensing
- sleep/wake context
- vital-sign-related sensing where technically feasible
- sleep-stage-related sensing where technically feasible and validated

The safe product truth is that Orisen is non-wearable/non-contact for the core experience.

Do not claim Orisen currently matches or beats wearables, replaces PSG, accurately detects sleep stages, or provides validated vital-sign accuracy unless evidence supports it.

## Autonomy boundary

Autonomous intervention is a possible future direction.

It should mean carefully permissioned, tested, and user-trusted automation, not uncontrolled behavior.

Potential future autonomous behaviors may include:

- adapting wake sequence intensity
- changing audio/light timing
- selecting a wake routine based on context
- adjusting re-trigger logic within user-approved boundaries
- suggesting or running bedtime routines

Avoid implying that Orisen already autonomously optimizes sleep, controls sleep stages, or fixes sleep without user input.

## Relationship to existing product truth

This direction strengthens existing product truth rather than replacing it.

Stable truths remain:

- Orisen is not primarily a passive sleep tracker.
- Orisen is not a wearable.
- Orisen is not currently a medical device.
- Wake completion is the first wedge.
- Reliability comes before intelligence.
- The final wake-up moment should run locally.
- Claims must match evidence.

## Safe internal shorthand

Use internally:

- hardware layer for AI-powered sleep intervention
- hardware layer for sleep software
- software-defined sleep interventions
- future AI sleep-agent interface
- non-wearable sleep-context sensing
- voice as an intervention channel

Use carefully:

- AI sleep agents
- autonomous sleep intervention
- sleep OS for the bedroom
- physical layer for sleep AI

Avoid as hard product truth unless later validated:

- proven AI sleep agent
- autonomous sleep optimization
- accurate vital-sign tracking
- accurate sleep-stage tracking
- sleep-stage control
- fixes all sleep
- replaces wearables
- replaces clinical sleep tracking

## Downstream impact

Marketing and fundraising can use this direction to explain the larger vision, but they must still separate:

- current wedge: presence-based wake completion
- current build: connected device/app loop and local wake behavior
- near-term roadmap: gradual audio/light and sensor-informed wake intervention
- future direction: voice, AI sleep agents, autonomous intervention, vital-sign/sleep-stage sensing
- unsupported claims: clinical outcomes, perfect sleep-stage detection, sleep-stage control, guaranteed physiological improvement
