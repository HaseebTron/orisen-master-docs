## Current target customer summary

Orisen is for people with a strong, repeated, painful wake-up problem.

The first users should not be casual sleep-optimization users who only want sleep graphs. The first users should be people who already feel that normal alarms, phone alarms, and willpower-based solutions are not enough.

## Current beachhead hypothesis

The current broad hypothesis is that Orisen should start with users who have high pain around waking up reliably and high consequences when they fail to wake up.

Possible early segments include:

- Chronic oversleepers
- People who sleep through alarms
- People who turn alarms off and go back to bed
- People with severe morning grogginess or sleep inertia
- Students with high consequences for missing classes, exams, or commitments
- Early-career professionals with high consequences for being late
- Shift workers or people with inconsistent schedules
- Performance-focused users or biohackers willing to try new sleep technology

This list is not final. It should be narrowed using evidence.

## Who Orisen is not for first

Orisen should not be designed first for:

- Casual sleep trackers
- People who only want sleep scores or dashboards
- People with mild interest but low pain
- Users who mainly want a prettier alarm clock
- Users who are unwilling to pay for wake-up reliability
- Medical users requiring clinical diagnosis or treatment claims

## Pain-level criteria

A strong early customer likely has several of these traits:

- They frequently oversleep or almost oversleep.
- They sleep through alarms or dismiss them while half-asleep.
- They return to bed after turning off alarms.
- They experience strong grogginess or sleep inertia.
- Being late has real consequences.
- They have already tried multiple alarm solutions.
- They are embarrassed, frustrated, or stressed by their mornings.
- They would pay for a reliable solution.

## Evidence needed

To narrow the first customer segment, collect evidence on:

- Which segment has the strongest pain
- Which segment understands the product fastest
- Which segment has the highest willingness to pay
- Which segment is easiest to reach
- Which segment is most likely to test a prototype
- Which segment gives the clearest feedback
- Which segment has the strongest referral potential
