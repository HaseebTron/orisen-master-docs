# Outreach Email Accounts

## Purpose

This file defines which email account should be used for Orisen fundraising and investor-related outreach.

The goal is to keep outreach professional, credible, distinctive, and easy to manage.

## Available email accounts

| Email | Type | Best Use | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca` | University email | Warm or semi-warm outreach where the University of Waterloo signal may increase trust, especially Waterloo alumni, professors, Velocity/UWaterloo ecosystem contacts, technical founders, Canadian tech people, and first-touch emails where differentiation matters | Can stand out in a crowded investor inbox and can create instant credibility, but should not make Orisen feel like only a student project. |
| `haseeb@orisen.ai` | Company email | Company credibility, formal investor threads, follow-ups after interest, deck sharing, calendar coordination, ongoing investor communication | Best for showing Orisen is a real company. Strong account to CC when first-touch email comes from Waterloo. |
| `ahaseeb2002@gmail.com` | Personal email | Existing personal relationships, informal contacts, backup account | Not ideal as the default investor outreach email because it looks less company-specific. |

## Current recommendation

Use a two-track approach instead of one universal default.

### Default for warm or semi-warm investor outreach

Use `abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca` as the sender when the Waterloo signal may help the email get opened or trusted.

CC `haseeb@orisen.ai` when the thread is investor-facing and you want to show company legitimacy.

This is especially useful when reaching out to:

- Waterloo alumni
- Velocity people
- Canadian tech investors
- Technical founders/operators
- People who may respect Waterloo engineering
- Semi-warm contacts who do not yet know Orisen well

Reason:

- The Waterloo domain can differentiate the email from a generic founder/company cold email.
- It gives the recipient an instant credibility hook.
- CCing the Orisen email shows that this is still a real company, not just a school project.
- It lets future investor replies include the company inbox early in the thread.

### Default for cold institutional VC outreach

Use `haseeb@orisen.ai` as the sender for colder, more formal, or institutional VC outreach unless the Waterloo signal is clearly relevant.

Reason:

- Some investors may expect a company email for formal fundraising.
- It avoids making Orisen look too student-project-like.
- It keeps the thread cleaner if the relationship becomes serious.

### Default for existing personal relationships

Use `ahaseeb2002@gmail.com` only when the recipient personally knows that account or the relationship is informal.

## Practical decision rule

Ask this before sending:

```text
Would the Waterloo name/domain make this person more likely to open, trust, or respond?
```

If yes:

```text
Send from abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca and CC haseeb@orisen.ai.
```

If no, and the email is investor-facing:

```text
Send from haseeb@orisen.ai.
```

If the person knows the founder personally through Gmail:

```text
Send from ahaseeb2002@gmail.com or move them to haseeb@orisen.ai after the first reply.
```

## When to use the Waterloo email

Use `abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca` when the Waterloo association meaningfully helps the outreach.

Good cases:

- Contacting Waterloo alumni
- Contacting Velocity-related people
- Contacting professors or university-linked advisors
- Asking for intros through university or alumni networks
- Contacting Canadian tech people where Waterloo engineering is a useful signal
- First-touch outreach where a non-generic sender domain may help the email stand out

Suggested pattern:

- Send from `abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca` when the Waterloo context is a credibility hook.
- CC `haseeb@orisen.ai` when the thread is investor-facing.
- In the body, still position yourself as founder of Orisen.
- Use the signature to connect both credibility signals: founder + Waterloo.
- If the conversation becomes formal fundraising, move the thread fully to `haseeb@orisen.ai` or keep it CCed.

## When to use the Orisen company email

Use `haseeb@orisen.ai` when company legitimacy matters more than school credibility.

Good cases:

- Formal VC outreach
- Follow-ups after an investor shows interest
- Sending the deck after a call
- Serious diligence conversations
- Calendar coordination for investor meetings
- Any thread where Orisen should look like the primary institution

## When to use the Gmail account

Use `ahaseeb2002@gmail.com` for personal relationships or very informal outreach.

Good cases:

- Friends
- Family friends
- People who know the founder personally through that account
- Situations where the recipient already recognizes the Gmail address

Avoid using Gmail as the default for cold investor outreach unless there is a specific reason.

## Recommended operating workflow

1. Use `abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca` + CC `haseeb@orisen.ai` for Waterloo-relevant warm or semi-warm outreach.
2. Use `haseeb@orisen.ai` for formal investor outreach, serious follow-ups, and ongoing fundraising threads.
3. Use `ahaseeb2002@gmail.com` only for personal-network conversations or backup.
4. Track every contact in `fundraising/investor-crm.md` regardless of which account was used.
5. Add a note in the CRM when a specific account was used and why.

## CRM note convention

When updating `fundraising/investor-crm.md`, use this format in the `Notes` column when email account choice matters:

```text
Sent from abdul.haseeb@uwaterloo.ca and CCed haseeb@orisen.ai because Waterloo signal may help.
```

or:

```text
Sent from haseeb@orisen.ai because this is formal investor outreach.
```

or:

```text
Sent from ahaseeb2002@gmail.com because this is a personal relationship.
```

## Caution

Do not overuse the Waterloo email in a way that makes Orisen feel like a class project or student side project.

The email should communicate:

```text
credible Waterloo-trained founder building a real company
```

not:

```text
student asking investors to look at a school idea
```
