## Stats for waking up

- Two in five Americans are ‘bad’ at mornings ([talkerresearch](https://talkerresearch.com/what-are-americans-morning-struggles/))
- 26% of people oversleep several times a week ([mattressinquirer](https://mattressinquirer.com/squandered-by-sleeping-in/))
- **45% of users were heavy snooze users** — pressing it ~4 times per session, for ~20 minutes daily ([Harvard / Nature Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-99563-y))

## Stats for difficulty falling asleep

- In 2024, 15.4% of adults had trouble falling asleep most days or every day ([CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/nchs/products/databriefs/db559.htm))
- Nearly 4 in 10 U.S. adults have trouble falling asleep 3+ nights per week ([National Sleep Foundation](https://www.thensf.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/NSF_SIA_2025-Report_final.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com))
- Two-thirds of Americans (62%) struggle to fall asleep each night ([studyfinds](https://studyfinds.com/next-day-anxiety-americans-struggle-sleep/))
