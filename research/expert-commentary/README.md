# Expert Commentary

## Purpose

This folder is the intended home for sleep-expert and domain-expert meeting notes.

Expert commentary is separate from `research/external-research/` because expert calls are primary founder-collected evidence, while papers, articles, Reddit posts, reviews, and market reports are external secondary sources.

## Evidence role

Expert commentary should be used for:

- claim discipline
- technical plausibility checks
- validation-design input
- roadmap realism
- market/ICP hypotheses to investigate
- expert warnings about overclaiming

Expert commentary should not be treated as proof that Orisen works unless the expert directly tested Orisen or reviewed Orisen-specific data.

## Claim boundaries

Safe:

- Expert commentary supports treating sleep-stage intervention as plausible but unvalidated.
- Expert commentary supports the need for empirical validation.
- Expert commentary supports caution around grogginess and sleep inertia claims.
- Expert commentary can suggest research topics such as spindles, arousal threshold, actigraphy, PSG, temperature, and light limitations.

Unsafe:

- Do not claim Orisen is clinically validated.
- Do not claim Orisen can manipulate sleep stages because an expert said it sounded possible.
- Do not claim Orisen reduces grogginess without Orisen-specific evidence.
- Do not claim an expert is a formal advisor, partner, or cofounder unless confirmed.
