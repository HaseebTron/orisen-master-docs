## AI-generated meeting summary pasted by founder

### Device Overview

- Abdul presented a sleep intervention device designed to eliminate oversleeping and grogginess by optimizing wake-up timing.
- The device uses millimeter radar technology to monitor vital signs non-contact and determine sleep stages.
- Core features include: transitioning users from deep sleep to light sleep before wake time using gradual audio, progressive light, warm/cool blue, and an alarm that will not stop until the user leaves the bed.
- MVP for guaranteed wake-up functionality has been developed and tested with pilot users.
- Sleep stage detection feature currently in development.

### Technical Discussion

- Wearable device accuracy against PSG is lower than expected: Oura Ring at 76-79%, Apple Watch 7 at 50-80%, Whoop at 50-61% for sleep stage classification.
- Wearables excel at sleep duration but struggle with accurate sleep staging compared to polysomnogram PSG gold standard.
- Dr. Benji suggested temperature as a critical additional variable for moving users out of deep/REM sleep into lighter stages.
- Discussed whether heart rate and breathing rate variance alone are sufficient for the wake-up optimization use case without requiring 70-80% sleep stage classification accuracy.

### Target Market and Positioning

- Target customers are young adults with rigid work schedules who struggle with waking up, not health optimization enthusiasts.
- Product positioned as consumer-grade rather than medical device to avoid certification requirements.
- Price point significantly lower than competitors like 8sleep to reduce friction.

### Research and Validation

- Sleep Health Centre South Africa offered clinical validation testing against PSG equipment in their three sleep labs.
- Cost approximately $250 USD per full overnight PSG with analysis.
- Dr. Benji emphasized that the hypothesis must be tested empirically, as subjective wake-up quality outcomes may not match theoretical mechanisms.
- Mentioned potential to test on specialized patient populations including hypersomnia and narcolepsy cases.

### Tools and Resources Discussed

- Open Evidence recommended as a database for published medical literature research.
- Amazon previously had a similar radar-based product that was discontinued.
- Loftie mentioned as a competitor with fixed wake-up transitions that receive mixed user feedback.
