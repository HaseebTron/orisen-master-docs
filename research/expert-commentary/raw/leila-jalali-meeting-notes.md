## Raw meeting notes

### Artificial sleep phase transitioning

- Artificial sleep phase transitioning sounds technically possible.
- She said disturbance can temporarily move a patient to a lighter sleep stage.
- In her testing/experience, when a patient was disturbed, they would temporarily move to a lighter phase, but then quickly shift back to their old stage after about a minute.
- Therefore, to keep someone in a lighter stage, Orisen would likely need to continuously apply disturbance/intervention.
- You cannot just shift them once and assume they will stay there.

### REM sleep and sleep timing

- About 70% of REM sleep is close to morning.
- About 20% to 25% of sleep is REM sleep.
- REM sleep brain activity looks exactly like when someone is awake.
- Sleep is a 24-hour thing, not just an 8-hour thing.

### Who struggles to wake up

- Mostly teens / young adults struggle with waking up.
- Founder note: she said roughly ages 14 to 23 struggle with waking up, around 80% in her clinic-style estimate.
- More screen time before sleep worsens this.
- When people grow up and go into industry, around age 23+, they often have a fixed sleep schedule, making it easier to wake up on time.
- Shift workers were another group, around 10% to 15% in her off-the-cuff estimate.
- Other groups: around 5% in her off-the-cuff estimate.
- She said she never had nurses or finance as a specific notable group.

### Grogginess and sleep inertia

- Grogginess:
  - People are not in a good mood.
  - People need coffee.
  - You cannot get rid of grogginess simply by shifting sleep stage.

- Sleep inertia:
  - Described as the urge to go back to sleep.
  - Mostly because of waking up in deep sleep, about 50% according to her off-the-cuff explanation.
  - Remaining causes include sleep quality, how much REM someone gets, what time they went to bed, and how much deep sleep they got.

### Best sleep phases to wake from

- N1 and REM are the best phases to wake up in.

### Snoozing

- Getting rid of repeated snoozing also gets rid of grogginess and inertia.

### Spindles

- Look into sleep spindles.
- If a person has spindles, it is hard for them to wake up.

### Patient categories by age / issue

- Young adults:
  - Consequences include losing their job, missing school, and parental conflict.
  - Usually 70% to 80% boys in her clinic-style estimate.

- Ages 25 to 40:
  - Sleep insomnia.
  - Difficulty falling asleep.
  - Waking up in the middle of the night.

- Middle age:
  - Sleep apnea.
  - Do not feel refreshed.
  - Snoring.

- Age 60+:
  - Wake up early and go to sleep early.
  - Opposite pattern of teenagers with delayed sleep phase.
